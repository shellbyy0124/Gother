ButtlerBot Jobs -- Law Enforcement Information

Law Enforcement

Law Enforcement are the ones who are typically your moderators and various levels of staff that are within your discord server. The Chart is as follows:
-Chief Of Police = Administrator
-Detectives = Moderators
-Police = Community Helpers
-Meter Maids = Trial Staff Member
Your Meter Maids are those who are being brought on, or hired, as staff and are in a 90 day probationary period. After 90 days, they will become regular Police and will advance from there.

Benefits

401K Percentage decided by the Chief Of Police and is taken out of everyones pay. This percentage rate can only be changed if all Chief of Police are available at the same time. Otherwise, a recess is to be called until all Chiefs are available.

Chief Of Police

The Chief Of Police are responsible for but are not limited to:
-Ensure laws are being enforced.
-Review reports received to determine disciplinary actions for members.
-Decides on 401k percentage.
-Paid a flat rate of $3,000 per case closed

Detectives

Detectives are responsible for investigating issues that arise within the discord. If there is an instance of let's say bullying to come about within one of the discord channels, then it is up to the detectives to pull each member into the interrogation room and find out what happened. Once all the facts have been discovered, the guilty and innocent party, and their record has been pulled, a report is then filled out and sent to the Chief of Police to further investigate and determine a course of action. If the case needs to go to court, then the Chief of Police will let the clerks know and a court date will be scheduled. If the member cannot be present for the court date and time, then a replacement date and time may be made within reason. They primary concern is the "Publics Safety". Detectives are paid $1,750 per case closed that they are assigned to.

Police

Police officers are the ones who get called when a person would dial 911. They're the ones who arrive on scene, get pictures, statements, etc that will be needed and will determine whether and investigation will be needed or not. After all required information has been obtained, the police officers will then write a report that will be sent either to the database and logged as a detective was not needed for the case, or will be sent to the detectives inbox for further investigation. Police officers are paid a flat $1,300 per case closed that they are assigned to.

Meter Maids

Meter Maids are the Police in training. When hired, Meter Maids must completed a mandatory 90 probationary period (real life timer set by Chief Of Police) in order to graduate to becoming a police officer. When a situation arises, Meter Maids duties and jobs are set by the Chief Of Police. Meter Maids are paid a salary of $500/week during training.

Clerks

Clerks are the ones who set date and times for court dates. They're also in charge of ensuring that fines are paid, warrants are issued, etc. The clerks fill out reports based on each court date that is being scheduled/rescheduled. Included in the reports are the number of times the current case number's court date and time has been rescheduled. At any given point, the clerk is allowed to no longer reschedule court dates.
